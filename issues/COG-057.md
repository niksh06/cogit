# COG-057: Durable removal provenance

Priority: P1 (the caller must provide a removal reason, but that reason is not
recoverable after the commit clears `index.json`; COG-045 therefore has to
infer superseded/refuted/retired structurally)

## Problem

`remove_fact` records `{id, reason}` only in the mutable index. A thought stores
its parents, resulting mindset, operation, message, author and timestamp, but
not the per-assertion removal reasons. After commit, the exact reason supplied
by the writer disappears.

For ordinary supersedes this is inconvenient; for refutation, retirement,
review and audit it loses provenance that Cogit explicitly required at write
time. Structural inference cannot recover free-form retirement reasons and can
be ambiguous around merge thoughts.

## Decision required

Write ADR-0014 before changing the format. Compare at least:

1. optional removal metadata on the thought object;
2. a dedicated transition/change object referenced by the thought;
3. a typed annotation overlay keyed to thought + assertion.

The recommendation should preserve atomic publication, human inspectability,
legacy readability and Python/Rust byte parity. It must define normal-commit
and two-parent merge semantics, recovery behavior, and whether the change is
an additive v1 extension or a future format version.

## Scope after the ADR decision

- Persist one reason for every removed assertion in the same atomic operation
  that publishes the new mindset.
- Expose it through log/diff/recap/dump/viewer and the lifecycle analytics.
- Keep structural classification as a fallback for legacy thoughts.
- Teach `verify` to cross-check recorded removals against the actual
  parent-to-mindset delta.

## Acceptance criteria

- ADR-0014 records the chosen representation, rejected alternatives, merge
  semantics, compatibility and recovery behavior before implementation.
- After a commit, each removed assertion's exact reason is recoverable without
  reading a transient index or parsing the thought message.
- `verify` rejects or warns on removal metadata inconsistent with the mindset
  delta, as specified by the ADR.
- Legacy repositories remain readable; old lifecycle analytics retain their
  structural fallback.
- Frozen vectors remain byte-for-byte unchanged. Any new vector and schema
  fields are explicitly approved by the ADR.
- Python/Rust and interop tests cover supersede, refute, retire and merge.
